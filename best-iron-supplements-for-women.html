Buying Guide

Choosing the right iron supplement depends on your specific situation: **Iron Form:** - **Ferrous (cheapest)**: Ferrous sulfate, fumarate, gluconate. Good absorption but common side effects. - **Chelated (gentler)**: Ferrous bisglycinate. Superior absorption and fewer digestive issues. Recommended for women. **Absorption Tips:** - Take iron with vitamin C (citrus, tomatoes) to increase absorption by 3-4x - Avoid taking with coffee, tea, milk, or calcium supplements (separate by 2+ hours) - Take on an empty stomach for best absorption, or with a small snack if nausea occurs **Dosage:** - Standard: 18-27mg elemental iron per day for premenopausal women - If deficient: 50-100mg elemental iron per day (consult doctor) **Side Effects:** - Constipation: Stay hydrated, eat fiber, try chelated forms - Nausea: Take with food or at night, reduce dose initially - Dark stools: Normal, not concerning

Frequently Asked Questions

🔹 How long does it take for iron supplements to work?

You may feel more energy within 2-4 weeks. Full iron stores replenish in 2-3 months. Blood tests can confirm levels.

🔹 Can I take iron with my other supplements?

Separate iron from calcium, magnesium, zinc, and dairy by at least 2 hours. Vitamin C enhances iron absorption—take together.

🔹 Are iron supplements safe long-term?

Iron is safe when taken as directed for documented deficiency. Get blood work to confirm deficiency before supplementing indefinitely.

🔹 What's the difference between ferrous and ferric iron?

Ferrous (Fe2+) is better absorbed and more bioavailable than ferric (Fe3+). Ferrous supplements are generally more effective.
