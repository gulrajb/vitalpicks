Buying Guide

What to Look For

  • Quality & Durability: Invest in equipment that will last years, not months.
  • Space Requirements: Measure your space before buying large equipment.
  • Weight Capacity: Ensure equipment supports your bodyweight plus additional load.
  • Adjustability: Look for equipment that grows with your fitness level.
  • Warranty: Good equipment comes with at least a 1-year warranty.

FAQ

Q: How much space do I need for a home gym?
A: A small home gym can fit in a spare bedroom (100-150 sq ft). For a full setup, 300+ sq ft is ideal.
Q: What equipment is best for beginners?
A: Start with dumbbells, resistance bands, and a yoga mat. Add equipment as you progress.
Q: Can I get a good workout at home?
A: Absolutely! With proper equipment and programming, home workouts are just as effective.
Q: How much should I spend on a home gym?
A: Start with $300-500 for basic equipment, scale up as budget allows.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Quality matters more than quantity
  • Start small, expand over time
  • Measure your space first
  • Read reviews before buying
