Buying Guide: What to Look For

1. Frequency Range

Devices typically operate at 1-3 MHz. Lower frequencies penetrate deeper (muscles), higher frequencies treat surface tissues.

2. Portability

Choose lightweight, battery-powered models for convenient home use and travel.

3. Treatment Modes

Look for adjustable intensity settings and pre-programmed protocols for different muscle groups.

4. FDA Clearance

Ensure the device is FDA-cleared as a Class II medical device for therapeutic use.

5. Warranty & Support

Quality devices come with 1-2 year warranties and access to customer support or instructional videos.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ultrasound therapy safe for home use?

Yes, FDA-cleared devices are safe when used per instructions. Always follow manufacturer guidelines and consult healthcare providers for specific conditions.

How often should I use ultrasound therapy?

Most protocols recommend 5-10 minute sessions, 1-2 times daily for acute injuries or chronic pain conditions.

Can ultrasound help with muscle soreness?

Yes, studies show ultrasound therapy accelerates recovery by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation in muscle tissues.

What areas can I treat?

Use on any accessible muscle or joint—shoulders, knees, ankles, back, and neck are common treatment areas.
