Buying Guide

Vitamin E is a fat-soluble antioxidant vital for skin and immune health. Choose mixed tocopherols for broader benefits than single-source d-alpha. Take with a meal for best absorption.

Key Factors to Consider:

  • Purity & Testing: Look for third-party tested products to ensure quality and safety.
  • Dosage & Form: Choose between capsules, powders, or gummies based on your preference and needs.
  • Bioavailability: Ensure the supplement is in a form your body can effectively absorb.
  • Brand Reputation: Established brands with customer reviews are generally more reliable.
  • Price Value: Compare cost per serving to find the best value for your budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between d-alpha and mixed tocopherols?
d-alpha is a single form, while mixed tocopherols include alpha, beta, gamma, and delta for broader antioxidant coverage.
How much Vitamin E should I take daily?
The RDA is 15 IU daily for adults. Most supplements range from 400-800 IU.
Can Vitamin E interact with medications?
High doses may interact with blood thinners. Consult your doctor if on anticoagulants.
