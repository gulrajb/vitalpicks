What is Quercetin & Why You Need It

Quercetin is a powerful plant-based bioflavonoid found in apples, onions, berries, and tea. It's a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory that supports immune function, reduces allergy symptoms, and promotes cardiovascular health. While you can get quercetin from food, supplements provide concentrated doses for therapeutic effects.

Key Benefits of Quercetin

  • Immune Support: Enhances immune cell function and response
  • Allergy Relief: Natural antihistamine properties reduce seasonal allergy symptoms
  • Anti-Inflammatory: Powerful antioxidant reduces inflammation throughout the body
  • Heart Health: Supports cardiovascular function and blood pressure
  • Respiratory Health: Beneficial for asthma and respiratory conditions
  • Athletic Recovery: Reduces exercise-induced inflammation

Quercetin Dosage Guide

Recommended Doses

  • General wellness: 500-1000mg daily
  • Allergy relief: 1000-1500mg daily, split into 2-3 doses
  • Athletic recovery: 1000mg post-workout
  • Immune boost: 500-1000mg daily during cold/flu season

Best Time to Take Quercetin

With meals: Fat and vitamin C enhance absorption, so take with food containing healthy fats (olive oil, nuts, avocado).

Daily consistency: Regular daily dosing provides best results for allergy relief and immune support.

Athletic use: Take 1-2 hours before exercise or immediately post-workout for anti-inflammatory benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is quercetin safe for daily use?

Yes, quercetin is exceptionally safe with minimal side effects. Studies support doses up to 1500mg daily long-term with no toxicity concerns.

Can quercetin cure allergies?

Quercetin is a natural antihistamine that relieves allergy symptoms but doesn't cure allergies. It works best as a preventative taken 1-2 weeks before allergy season.

Should I take quercetin with vitamin C?

Yes. Vitamin C significantly enhances quercetin absorption and potency. Many supplements combine them for this reason.

Does quercetin interact with medications?

Quercetin is generally safe, but high doses may interact with blood thinners and certain heart medications. Consult your doctor if on prescription medications.
