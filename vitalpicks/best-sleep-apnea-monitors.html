Buying Guide

Sleep apnea monitoring devices range from smartwatches to medical-grade wearables. Choose based on: 1) **Measurement capability** - SpO2 (oxygen saturation) is essential for detecting apnea events, 2) **Wear style** - rings, wristbands, or chest-worn devices, 3) **Medical clearance** - FDA approval for accuracy, 4) **Data logging** - records for sharing with your doctor, 5) **Alerts** - real-time notifications for dangerous oxygen drops, 6) **Integration** - smartphone app for trend analysis. Professional sleep studies remain gold standard, but home monitors help track progress and trends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Some premium smartwatches monitor SpO2 (blood oxygen), which can indicate apnea events. However, medical-grade devices with dedicated algorithms are more accurate for diagnosis.

Normal oxygen saturation is 95-100%. During sleep, mild dips to 90-94% are normal. Drops below 90% or frequent events warrant medical attention.

No prescription required for consumer wearables, but for medical-grade diagnostic devices, your doctor may need to order them for insurance coverage.

FDA-cleared home monitors achieve 85-95% accuracy compared to lab studies. They're excellent for tracking trends but don't replace clinical diagnosis.
