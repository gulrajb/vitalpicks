Buying Guide

How to Choose

For falling asleep: Valerian root or magnesium glycinate work best.

For staying asleep: Valerian root + passionflower combination is ideal.

For an anxious mind: Passionflower + L-theanine.

For gentle daily use: Chamomile tea or L-theanine.

Dosage & Timing

Take valerian and passionflower 30–60 minutes before bed. L-Theanine can be taken 20–30 minutes before. Start with the lowest dose and increase if needed after 2 weeks.

Safety Notes

Natural doesn't always mean safer. Avoid valerian if you're on sedatives. Passionflower may interact with blood thinners. Always check with your doctor if you're on other medications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are natural sleep aids addictive?

A: No. Valerian, passionflower, and L-theanine are non-habit forming. You won't develop dependence even with long-term use — unlike prescription sleeping pills.

Q: How long until they work?

A: L-Theanine works in 20–30 minutes. Valerian and passionflower take 2–4 weeks for full effect, but many people notice improvement within 3–5 days.

Q: Can I combine different sleep supplements?

A: Yes. Valerian + passionflower is a proven combo. You can also add magnesium. Just avoid combining multiple sedating herbs without professional guidance.

Q: Are they safe for daily use?

A: Yes. Valerian, passionflower, L-theanine, and chamomile are safe for daily use over months or years. No known long-term side effects.

Q: Will they make me groggy in the morning?

A: Valerian may cause mild grogginess if you're sensitive. L-Theanine and passionflower rarely do. Chamomile tea almost never does.

Final Verdict

Best overall: Valerian root for most people. Proven, affordable, effective.

Best for anxiety: Passionflower or L-Theanine combo.

Best for beginners: L-Theanine (no waiting period, works quickly, gentle).
