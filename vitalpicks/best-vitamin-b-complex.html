Buying Guide

Choose a methylated B-complex if you have MTHFR mutations or absorption issues. Look for B12 as methylcobalamin (not cyanocobalamin) for better retention. If you're sensitive to niacin flush, choose a "no-flush" niacin formula. Athletes benefit from extra B6 and B12 for energy and recovery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When should I take B-complex?
Take with breakfast for best absorption. B vitamins can increase energy, so avoid taking late afternoon or evening.
Q: Can I take B-complex every day?
Yes. B vitamins are water-soluble, so excess is excreted. Daily supplementation is safe and recommended.
Q: What's the difference between B vitamins?
B1 (thiamine) = energy, B2 (riboflavin) = cellular energy, B3 (niacin) = metabolism, B5 (pantothenic acid) = hormone function, B6 = neurotransmitters, B7 (biotin) = hair/skin, B9 (folate) = DNA, B12 = nerve function.
Q: Do I need methylated B vitamins?
If you have MTHFR mutations or poor methylation, yes. Otherwise, standard cyanocobalamin works fine.
Q: Best B-complex for energy?
Look for high B12 (1000+ mcg) and B6 (25-50mg). These directly support ATP production and mitochondrial function.
