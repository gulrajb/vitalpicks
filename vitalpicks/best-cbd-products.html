CBD Buying Guide

How to Choose the Right CBD Product

  1. Start with your goal — Pain, anxiety, sleep, general wellness? Choose a product tailored for it.
  2. Choose a delivery method — Oils for fast effect, gummies for convenience, topicals for targeted relief.
  3. Check third-party testing — Look for a QR code or lab report on the website.

  6. Track results — Keep a simple note of how you feel, sleep, pain levels, and mood.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CBD legal?
Yes. Hemp-derived CBD with less than 0.3% THC is federally legal in the US under the 2018 Farm Bill. Always check your local laws, as some states have restrictions.
Will CBD make me high?
No. CBD is non-intoxicating — it won't produce any high. You're thinking of THC, a different cannabinoid. CBD actually reduces the psychoactive effects of THC.
How long does CBD take to work?
Timing varies by delivery method: oils and tinctures (15–45 min), gummies (1–2 hours), topicals (20–30 min). Sublingual oils work fastest because they bypass digestion.
What's the best CBD dose?
There's no universal dose — it depends on your body weight, condition, and sensitivity. Start with 5–10mg daily and increase by 5mg weekly until you find your effective dose. Most people use 10–20mg daily.
Can I take CBD with medications?
CBD can interact with certain medications (especially those metabolized by the liver). Always consult your doctor or pharmacist before starting CBD, especially if you take blood thinners, anxiety meds, or sleep aids.
Is full-spectrum better than isolate?
Yes. Full-spectrum contains all cannabinoids and terpenes, which work synergistically (entourage effect). Isolate is pure CBD only. Full-spectrum is more effective but may contain trace THC.
Where should I buy CBD?
Stick to reputable brands with third-party lab testing. Avoid gas stations and unknown online sellers. Amazon, official brand websites, and verified retailers are your safest bets.
