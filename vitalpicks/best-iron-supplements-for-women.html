Buying Guide: How to Choose

Iron type matters: Ferrous bisglycinate and chelated forms are easier to absorb and gentler on the stomach than ferrous fumarate.

Added ingredients: Look for vitamin C, copper, and B vitamins which enhance absorption and utilization.

Dosage: Women aged 19-50 need 18mg daily (25mg if pregnant or heavy menstrual cycles).

FAQ

Q: Can I take iron with coffee or tea?
A: No, coffee, tea, and calcium inhibit iron absorption. Take iron 2 hours away from these.
Q: What are common side effects?
A: Constipation, nausea, and dark stools are common. Ferrous bisglycinate forms have fewer side effects.
Q: How long until I feel the effects?
A: Most women notice improved energy within 2-4 weeks of consistent supplementation.
Q: Should I get my iron levels tested first?
A: Yes, consult with a healthcare provider to check your baseline iron and ferritin levels before starting.
