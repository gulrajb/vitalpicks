Complete Buying Guide

1. Accuracy & Reliability

Look for FDA-cleared devices with clinical validation. Real-time monitoring should have minimal lag and high accuracy rates (±5mg/dL or better).

2. Ease of Use

Consider setup time, battery life, and app integration. The best devices sync seamlessly with your phone and require minimal calibration.

3. Cost & Insurance Coverage

Device prices vary widely. Some insurance plans cover CGMs and heart monitors; check your coverage before purchasing.

4. Data Privacy

Review privacy policies carefully. Your health data is sensitive — choose companies with transparent data handling practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Do I need a prescription?

Many CGMs and heart monitors require a doctor's prescription, while others (like Freestyle Libre) may not. Check product requirements.

❓ How often do I need to replace sensors?

Most sensors last 7-14 days and are disposable. Factor replacement costs into your budget.

❓ Can I use these while exercising?

Yes! Most modern devices are water-resistant and designed for active lifestyles. Check sweat and water resistance ratings.

❓ What's the difference between CGM and traditional glucose meters?

CGMs provide continuous data every 5 minutes, while traditional meters require fingerstick tests. CGMs offer better insights into patterns.
