🔬 The Science of Muscle Growth

Muscle growth (hypertrophy) requires three things: progressive overload in training, adequate protein intake (1.6–2.2g/kg bodyweight), and sufficient calories. Supplements cannot replace these fundamentals. However, creatine, protein powder, and caffeine are the three supplements with the strongest evidence for improving muscle-relevant outcomes.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need supplements to build muscle?

No. Supplements supplement a good diet and training program — they don't replace it. You can build significant muscle with food alone. Supplements just make it more convenient and slightly faster.

How fast can I build muscle?

Naturally, men can gain 0.5–1kg of muscle per month in the first year of training. Women gain roughly half that. After 2–3 years, progress slows significantly.

Is protein powder bad for kidneys?

No, in healthy individuals. The kidney damage myth comes from studies on people with pre-existing kidney disease. If your kidneys are healthy, up to 3g/kg of protein daily is safe.
