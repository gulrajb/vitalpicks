🛒 How to Pick Supplements as a Runner

Prioritize basics before anything fancy. Most runners are deficient in the fundamentals.

Start with the Basics

Iron, vitamin D, magnesium, and omega-3 deficiencies are the most common in runners and have the most impact on performance and recovery. Fix these first before spending on anything else.

Caffeine is the Best Legal Performance Drug

200mg of caffeine 30–60 minutes before a run can improve endurance by 10–15%. Use it strategically on hard effort days.

Recovery is Where Gains Are Made

Protein (20–40g within 2 hours of running) and tart cherry extract (for inflammation) are the two most underused recovery tools among recreational runners.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the single best supplement for runners?

If you could only take one, make it omega-3 (fish oil). It reduces inflammation, protects joints, supports heart health, and aids recovery. Every runner benefits.

Do I need protein powder as a runner?

Not necessarily, but it helps if you struggle to hit 1.4–1.7g of protein per kg of bodyweight through food. Post-run protein speeds up muscle repair and adaptation.

Is beetroot juice worth it?

Yes — if you want a legal performance edge. Studies show beetroot extract improves time to exhaustion by 15% and lowers the oxygen cost of running. Take 500mg of beetroot extract 2–3 hours before your race or hard run.
