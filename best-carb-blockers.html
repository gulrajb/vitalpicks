What Are Carb Blockers?

Carb blockers are supplements that contain enzymes or compounds designed to inhibit the digestion of carbohydrates, potentially reducing the number of carbs your body absorbs. The most common active ingredient is white kidney bean extract, which contains an enzyme inhibitor called alpha-amylase inhibitor.

How Do Carb Blockers Work?

Carb blockers work by slowing down the breakdown of complex carbohydrates into glucose, which delays their absorption. This can help reduce blood sugar spikes and may support weight management when combined with a healthy diet.

When to Take Carb Blockers

Take carb blockers 15–30 minutes before a carb-heavy meal for best results. They're most effective with starchy foods like rice, pasta, and bread. They won't affect protein or fats.

Do Carb Blockers Actually Work?

Research supports carb blockers' effectiveness, but results vary. Studies on Phase 2 show 25-75% carb reduction depending on dosage and meal composition. However, they're not a magic pill — diet and exercise still matter most.

Side Effects

Carb blockers are generally safe, but some people experience mild bloating or digestive discomfort as undigested carbs pass through. Start with a lower dose to assess tolerance.

Bottom Line

Phase 2 carb blockers work best when combined with a healthy diet and exercise routine. They're particularly useful if you struggle with carb cravings or need blood sugar management support. Choose a standardized Phase 2 extract for the most reliable results.
