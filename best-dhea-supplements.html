What Is DHEA?

DHEA is a hormone precursor produced by the adrenal glands. It naturally peaks in your 20s and declines with age. DHEA supplements may support energy levels, mood, sexual function, and body composition when used appropriately.

How DHEA Works

DHEA converts to testosterone and estrogen in your body, depending on what your system needs. This makes it a "balancing" hormone that supports multiple systems. Levels decline significantly after age 30, which is why supplementation appeals to older adults.

DHEA Dosing

Standard doses range from 25-50mg daily. Start low and monitor how you feel. DHEA levels vary by individual, and optimal dosing depends on your baseline hormones.

Who Should Use DHEA?

DHEA is best suited for adults over 40 with declining energy, sexual function, or mood. If you have hormone-sensitive conditions (breast cancer, prostate cancer, etc.), consult a doctor before use.

Potential Side Effects

Most people tolerate DHEA well at standard doses. Potential side effects include acne, mood changes, and hair growth in sensitive areas. These are usually reversible upon discontinuation.

Bottom Line

DHEA can be a valuable tool for age-related hormone support, but it's not a magic supplement. Start with 25mg, monitor your response, and consider consulting a functional medicine doctor to check your baseline DHEA levels before starting.
