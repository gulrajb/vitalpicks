🔬 What Actually Works for Sleep?

Magnesium Glycinate — the most evidence-backed sleep supplement. Relaxes muscles, calms the nervous system, and improves sleep quality significantly. Most people are deficient.

Melatonin (low dose) — 0.5–3mg helps reset your circadian rhythm. Most products overdose at 10mg, which causes grogginess. Less is more.

L-Theanine — an amino acid from green tea. Promotes relaxation without drowsiness and works especially well combined with low-dose melatonin.

💡 How to Use Sleep Supplements

Timing matters

Take magnesium 30–60 minutes before bed. Take melatonin 30 minutes before your target sleep time. Don't take both right before getting into bed — they need time to absorb.

Start low

With melatonin especially, start at 0.5–1mg. Most products sell 10mg doses which cause next-day grogginess. Bigger is not better here.

Fix the basics first

No supplement fixes a bright phone screen at midnight, a 3pm coffee habit, or a hot bedroom. Sort those first — supplements enhance good sleep hygiene, they don't replace it.
