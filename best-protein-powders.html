🧠 How to Choose the Right Protein Powder

1. Know Your Goal First

Muscle gain: Prioritize protein per serving (24g+). ON Gold Standard or Dymatize ISO100 are ideal. Timing matters less than total daily protein — aim for 0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight.

Weight loss: Go for a low-carb isolate under 5g carbs per serving. Dymatize ISO100 is the best option here.

Everyday health: Any quality concentrate works — MyProtein gives you 80% of the benefit at half the price.

2. Always Check the Label

Check protein per 100g — it should be above 70g. Anything lower means you're paying for fillers, sugars, or cheap fats. Avoid products with "proprietary blends" in the protein section — that's a way to hide under-dosing.

3. Third-Party Testing Matters

The supplement industry is largely unregulated. Look for certifications from NSF Certified for Sport, Informed Sport, or Labdoor. These guarantee the label matches what's actually in the product — and that it's free of banned substances.

4. Don't Overpay

Premium packaging and celebrity endorsements don't improve muscle protein synthesis. MyProtein delivers comparable results to brands charging 3x the price. Save money on protein and spend it on whole foods instead.
