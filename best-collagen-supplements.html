🔬 Does Collagen Actually Work?

Short answer: yes — but only if you buy the right type and take it consistently. Most cheap collagen supplements use low-quality sources with poor absorption. The key is hydrolyzed collagen peptides — broken down into smaller molecules your gut can actually use.

📚 What the Research Says

Multiple randomized controlled trials have shown hydrolyzed collagen can improve skin elasticity and hydration after 8–12 weeks of daily use, and reduce joint pain in athletes after 24 weeks. The effective dose is 10–15g per day. Results take time — don't expect miracles in 2 weeks.

💡 How to Take Collagen for Best Results

1. Take it every single day

Collagen is not a quick fix. Studies that show real results used collagen for 8–12 weeks of daily supplementation. Missing days significantly slows results. The easiest habit is adding it to your morning coffee or smoothie — it's tasteless, so you won't notice it.

2. Take it with Vitamin C

Vitamin C is a cofactor for collagen synthesis in your body. Either buy a collagen supplement with added Vitamin C (like Vital Proteins), or take 500mg of Vitamin C alongside your collagen. Without enough Vitamin C, your body can't properly use the collagen you're consuming.

3. Use the right dose

Research supports 10–15g daily for skin and joint benefits. Most supplements provide 10–18g per scoop — one scoop per day is the standard. More isn't necessarily better once you hit 15g.

4. Be patient

Skin improvements are typically seen at 8 weeks. Joint pain reduction takes 12–24 weeks of consistent use. This is a long-game supplement — don't quit after 3 weeks because you don't see changes yet.
