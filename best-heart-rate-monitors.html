Buying Guide

Choose a chest strap (Polar H10) if you want the most accurate heart rate data for serious training, interval workouts, or running. Medical-grade accuracy.

Choose a sports watch (Garmin) if you want GPS tracking, multiple sports modes, and advanced training metrics all in one device.

Choose a smartwatch (Apple Watch) if you use iPhone and want daily wear convenience plus health monitoring.

Choose a fitness tracker (Fitbit) if you're on a budget and want basic activity and sleep tracking without fancy features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are chest strap monitors more accurate than smartwatch monitors?

Yes. Chest straps read electrical signals directly from your heart and are typically ±2-5 bpm accurate. Optical sensors on smartwatches use light to estimate HR and can vary ±10-15 bpm, especially during high-intensity exercise or if you have darker skin tones.

Can I use any heart rate monitor with my phone?

Most modern monitors use Bluetooth and work with iOS and Android apps like Strava, TrainingPeaks, and Apple Health. Check compatibility before buying.

How long do heart rate monitor batteries last?

Chest straps: 300-400 hours. Smartwatches: 4-14 days. Fitness trackers: 10-21 days.

Which heart rate monitor is best for swimming?

Both chest straps and smartwatches can be used, but check waterproof ratings. Most are rated to 30-50m depth. Apple Watch and Garmin sports watches are excellent for pool training.

Do I need a heart rate monitor if I have a smartwatch?

If your smartwatch has an optical heart rate monitor, it's fine for casual fitness. But if you do high-intensity training, running, or cycling seriously, a chest strap gives much better data.
