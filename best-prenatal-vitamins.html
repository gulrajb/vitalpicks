Buying Guide

When selecting a prenatal vitamin, prioritize these key nutrients: - **Folic Acid (400-800 mcg)**: Prevents neural tube defects. Methylfolate is better absorbed. - **Iron (27mg)**: Supports increased blood volume during pregnancy. - **DHA (200-300mg)**: Supports fetal brain and eye development. - **Calcium (1000mg)**: May need a separate supplement due to tablet size constraints. - **Vitamin D (600 IU)**: Essential for calcium absorption and immune health. Start taking prenatal vitamins at least one month before conception if possible. If you experience nausea, try taking with food or switching to a different formulation. Always consult your OB/GYN before starting any supplement.

Frequently Asked Questions

🔹 When should I start taking prenatal vitamins?

Ideally, start 1-3 months before trying to conceive. If already pregnant, start immediately. The first trimester is critical for fetal development.

🔹 Can prenatal vitamins cause weight gain?

Prenatal vitamins themselves don't cause weight gain. Weight gain during pregnancy is normal and necessary. Weight management should be discussed with your doctor.

🔹 Are gummy prenatal vitamins effective?

Gummy prenatals can be effective, but check the label for adequate iron content. Some gummies have lower iron due to formulation challenges.

🔹 Can I take prenatal vitamins while breastfeeding?

Yes, many prenatal vitamins are safe while breastfeeding. Check with your doctor, as some nutrients may need adjustment post-pregnancy.
