About Us

Honest Health Reviews You Can Actually Trust

VitalPicks was built out of frustration. We got tired of health review sites that ranked products based on who paid the most for placement — not which products actually work. So we built something different.

Our Mission

We believe you deserve honest, research-backed information before spending your hard-earned money on supplements and fitness gear. Our mission is simple: test products thoroughly, explain what the science says, and give you a clear recommendation — even if that recommendation is "skip it."

What Makes Us Different

🔬

Science First

Every claim we make is backed by peer-reviewed research. We link to the studies so you can verify everything yourself.

💸

No Pay-to-Play

Brands cannot pay us to rank their products higher. Our editorial is completely independent of our affiliate revenue.

⚖️

Real Cons

We always include genuine drawbacks. If a product is overpriced or overhyped, we say so clearly.

🔄

Kept Current

We revisit every review every 3–6 months. Formulas change, new research drops, and prices shift — we stay current.

How We Test & Review Products

1

We research the category

Before touching a product, we read the latest clinical research on the ingredient category — what doses work, what doesn't, and what the studies actually say versus what marketing claims.

2

We analyze ingredients & labels

We check every supplement's label for proprietary blends (a red flag), under-dosed ingredients, unnecessary fillers, and third-party testing certifications.

3

We test the top contenders

For products we can physically test — protein powders, fitness trackers, gym equipment — we buy them ourselves and use them for 4–8 weeks minimum before reviewing.

4

We cross-reference user feedback

We analyze thousands of verified Amazon reviews to find patterns in real-world performance that lab testing can't capture.

5

We give you our honest pick

After all of that, we give you our clear recommendation — the best overall, the best budget option, and who should skip the category entirely.
